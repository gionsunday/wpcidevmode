Program Initiatives

Scientific Research

We lead community-based research to understand how women perceive cancer, access care, and respond to screening. Our findings shape every program we run. We translate global cancer data into clear, actionable information Nigerian women can use—because knowledge saves lives.

Capacity Development

In partnership with Project ECHO and other institutions, we train healthcare workers in early detection. Our field training, webinars, and seminars ensure professionals are equipped to fight cancer, ultimately reducing mortality rates through preventive education and treatment access.

Health Education

WCPI educates women on cancer prevention and early detection. We simplify complex information and deliver it in ways that are accessible and relevant—beginning in Remo North, Ogun State, and now reaching communities across Nigeria.

Cancer Screening

We offer free mammograms and cervical screenings during outreach programs. Every woman screened receives comprehensive care including consultations, multivitamins, and referrals if needed—empowering them to act early and protect their health.

HPV Vaccination Advocacy

To reduce cervical cancer, we advocate for full HPV vaccine coverage through school-based programs, local campaigns, and free vaccine drives. We aim to protect every Nigerian girl, no matter her background or location.
