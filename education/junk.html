Our Partnerships

Collaborating for Impact: Strengthening Our Reach

Over the years, WCPI has built strong partnerships with government agencies, healthcare providers, and organizations that share our mission of reducing the burden of cancer on women. These collaborations have been instrumental in our outreach efforts, expanding our reach, and making a tangible difference in the lives of many Nigerian women.

Trusted Partners

We are proud to have worked with esteemed institutions such as:

  • National Institute for Cancer Research and Treatment (NICRAT)
  • Clinton Health Access Initiative (CHAI)
  • Lagos State Ministry of Health
  • Lagos University Teaching Hospital (LUTH)
  • Breast Imaging Society of Nigeria
  • Faith-based organizations and other NGOs

Our partnership with Lagos University Teaching Hospital and other academic centers ensures that women who need treatment are referred to specialized cancer care providers. We also work alongside CHAI to ensure cost-effective, life-saving treatments are accessible.

Mobile Screening Units: Taking Services to the People

WCPI’s mobile mammography unit has become a critical part of our outreach. In partnership with diagnostic centers, we've expanded access to screenings by taking state-of-the-art diagnostic equipment directly to underserved communities. Our mobile units have reached over 7,000 women and we aim to scale this across all six geopolitical zones in Nigeria.

A Call for Future Partnerships

As we continue to grow, WCPI remains open to collaborations that align with our mission. Whether through funding, knowledge sharing, or logistical support—we welcome all forms of partnership.

Together with our trusted partners, we can make a significant impact in the fight against cancer.
