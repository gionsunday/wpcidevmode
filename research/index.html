Data in Motion: How WCPI Is Redefining Cancer Research in Nigeria

At the Women Cancer Prevention Initiative (WCPI), we know that effective cancer prevention doesn’t begin in clinics—it begins with the right information. That’s why we’re not just offering services. We’re gathering evidence, analyzing patterns, and asking the critical questions that shape smarter, more sustainable care for women across Nigeria.

From Field to Insight: Real Research in Real Communities

In 2024, WCPI led a feasibility pilot study on breast cancer screening across several health facilities in Lagos and Abuja. We tested an important question: what happens when we stop asking women to come to care—and start taking care of them?

The answer was clear. When access meets proximity, women show up.

Our research showed that mobile mammography is not only feasible, but it is transformative.


3,776 women enrolled in the study over a few months

2,836 of them screened through our mobile mammography van—demonstrating faster, more consistent uptake than fixed-location equipment

Monthly screening rates grew to over 700 women per month, far exceeding our original target of 2,000 within four months

Findings confirmed that barriers to screening are driven more by cost and accessibility than fear or lack of awareness

This research has already begun shaping the future of our programs—and others.

A Vision Built on Data

Our mobile screening model isn’t just a service—it’s a research-backed strategy designed to scale. What we’ve learned has confirmed that if we want to reach more Nigerian women, especially those in rural or underserved areas, we must stay mobile, community-rooted, and data-driven.

And this is only the beginning.

We are now moving toward the next phase of our research journey:

Expand our mobile screening program to all six geopolitical zones of Nigeria
Scale up data collection to reach 70,000–100,000 women across the country
Launch a longitudinal study to track sequential mammograms and develop an AI-powered predictive model for breast cancer risk in Nigerian women
Partner with U.S. academic institutions at the forefront of artificial intelligence in breast imaging to train these models using real, localized data
Collaborate with the Nigerian Federal Government to build a national implementation plan based on our findings—one that brings evidence into policy, and policy into practice

This is not just research. It’s a blueprint for what cancer care in Nigeria can and should look like: inclusive, proactive, and centered around the women who need it most.

Why This Matters

Too often, African women are excluded from global cancer studies. WCPI is changing that by building one of Nigeria’s most robust, community-informed breast cancer research platforms—designed by Africans, for Africans. We are generating the data that will, not only improve care today but also, drive the innovation, policy, and prevention strategies of tomorrow.

Because the future of Women health in Nigeria deserves to be built on evidence. And every data point we collect brings us closer to a country where fewer women die from preventable cancers.
